# Core Framework
- The Quantitative Value framework identifies undervalued companies while filtering out firms with high financial risk or earnings manipulation.

## 5 Pillars:
# 1. Value
- Measures: EV/EBIT, earnings yield
- Goal: Find cheap companies
- Risk: Cheap can mean “bad business”

# 2. Quality
- Measures: profitability, margins
- Goal: Ensure strong operations

# 3. Financial Strength
- Measures: leverage, liquidity
- Goal: Avoid fragile firms

# 4. Earnings Manipulation (Beneish)
- Measures: accruals, accounting signals
- Goal: Detect potential fraud
- Key insight: High PMAN = red flag

# 5. Distress Risk
- Measures: bankruptcy likelihood
- Goal: Avoid firms that may fail

## Combined Insight
- Best investments = cheap + high quality + low risk
- Worst = cheap + fraud/distress risk

## Interpretations: 
- Interpretation:
  - PMAN < 0.2 → low risk
  - PMAN 0.2–0.5 → moderate risk
  - PMAN > 0.5 → high manipulation risk
  
- Interpretation:
  - < 10x → cheap
  - 10–20x → reasonable
  - > 25x → expensive


### Decision Rules

- Cheap + High Quality + Low Risk → Strong buy signal
- Cheap + Low Quality → Value trap risk
- Cheap + High PMAN → Avoid (possible manipulation)
- Expensive + High Quality → Growth stock (not value)
- High Distress → Avoid regardless of valuation
